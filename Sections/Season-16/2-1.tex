\SSpsetQ{
  Find the sum of all integers \(x\) such that 
  \begin{equation*}
    3^{2x}+5^{2x}=34\cdot15^{x-1}
  \end{equation*}
}\bigskip

\begin{solution}\hfil\medskip
  
We have a quadratic in \(3^x\) and also \(5^x\). Taking it as a quadratic in \(3^x\) gives \(\left(3^x-\frac{17}{15}\cdot5^x\right)^2-\left(\frac{15^2-17^2}{15^2}\right)5^{2x}=0\), this gives \(3^x=\frac{1}{3}\cdot 5^{x+1}\) or \(3^x=3\cdot5^{x-1}\). Thus we see the only possible values of \(x\) are \(\pm 1\). So the answer is \fbox{0}
\end{solution}\bigskip
